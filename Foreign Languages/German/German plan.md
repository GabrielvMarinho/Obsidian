### **1. Master the Basics of German Sentence Structure**

- **Verb-second rule** (in main clauses): _Ich gehe morgen zur Schule._
    
- **Verb-last rule** (in subordinate clauses): _...weil ich morgen zur Schule gehe._
    
- Learn simple sentence construction: **Subject + Verb + Object.**
    

---

### **2. Learn Noun Genders and Articles**

- Understand **der (masc.), die (fem.), das (neut.)**
    
- Practice **definite vs. indefinite articles**: _der Mann, ein Mann_
    
- Learn about **plural forms**: _die Männer, die Frauen_
    

---

### **3. Understand Verb Conjugation (Present Tense)**

- Regular verbs: _machen (to do), spielen (to play)_
    
- Irregular verbs: _sein (to be), haben (to have), gehen (to go), sehen (to see)_
    
- Pronouns: ich, du, er/sie/es, wir, ihr, sie/Sie
    

---

### **4. Learn the Four Cases (Nominative, Accusative, Dative, Genitive)**

- **Nominative** = subject
    
- **Accusative** = direct object
    
- **Dative** = indirect object
    
- **Genitive** = possession  
    Example: _Der Mann gibt dem Kind einen Ball._
    

---

### **5. Modal Verbs and Sentence Structure**

- Common modal verbs: _können (can), müssen (must), wollen (want), dürfen (may), sollen (should)_
    
- Word order: Modal verb in second position, main verb at the end  
    Example: _Ich kann Deutsch sprechen._
    

---

### **6. Learn Separable and Inseparable Prefix Verbs**

- Separable: _aufstehen (to get up)_ → _Ich stehe früh auf._
    
- Inseparable: _verstehen (to understand)_ → _Ich verstehe dich._
    

---

### **7. Get Comfortable with Past Tenses**

- **Perfekt** (spoken past): _Ich habe gegessen._
    
- **Präteritum** (written past): _Ich aß._ (more common in books or with “sein”/“haben”)
    
- Learn auxiliary verb use: _haben_ or _sein_
    

---

### **8. Learn Adjective Endings and Declension**

- Adjective endings depend on gender, case, and article  
    Example: _ein schöner Tag, eine schöne Blume, ein schönes Haus_
    

---

### **9. Dive into Subordinating Conjunctions and Word Order**

- Common conjunctions: _weil, dass, obwohl, wenn_
    
- They send the verb to the **end** of the clause:  
    _Ich bleibe zu Hause, weil es regnet._
    

---

### **10. Practice with Reflexive Verbs and Passive Voice**

- Reflexive: _sich waschen, sich freuen_  
    → _Ich wasche mich._
    
- Passive voice:  
    → _Der Brief wird geschrieben._ (The letter is being written)
